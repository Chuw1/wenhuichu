Undergraduate/Graduate course, Texas A&M University, Computer Science and Engineering Department, Summer 2025

Credits: 3. 3 Lecture Hours. Study of the fundamental concepts and design principles of modern operating systems. Topics include process and thread management, scheduling, synchronization, deadlock, memory management, file systems, input/output systems, and virtualization.

The course emphasizes how operating systems manage hardware resources and provide abstractions to applications. Students will learn how modern OS kernels are structured and how concurrency, memory, and storage are coordinated to achieve performance, reliability, and security.

Prerequisite: Grade of C or better in CSCE 312 or equivalent knowledge of computer organization and assembly-level programming.
